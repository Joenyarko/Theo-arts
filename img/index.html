ARTIST STATEMENT


As a contemporary mixed media art, Theodora creates introspective and emotive works that explore the complexities of mental health and human experience. Inspired by her own struggles with anxiety and depression, her art delves into the realms of emotional vulnerability, resilience, and hope.

Through the combination of painting and sculpture, Norkai aims to capture the fragmented nature of mental states and the ongoing quest for healing and self-discovery. Theodora's artistic vision is shaped by her belief in the power of creative expression as a therapeutic tool and a means to foster empathy and understanding.

As a mental health advocate, Theodora draws inspiration from the complexities of the mind, deliberately omitting some body parts in her figures (paintings and sculptures) to symbolize unseen struggles like depression and anxiety.


Through her creative journey, Theodora Norkai thrives to explore mental health journeys, share experiences of people navigating mental challenges in today's society, and share stories of resilience, coping strategies, and help destigmatize the stigma that surrounds mental health illness and victims.

About me

ARTIST

I am Theodora Norkai Tetteh (born, September 2001). I am a comtemporary artist and a paper sculptor from Accra, Ghana. I am passionate about exploring issues related to mental health, its importance and effects. I try to depict them in my works.
I incorporate art techniques such as painting and paper mache into the creation of my pieces to communicate with my audience on a visceral level.
After studying and working as a graphic designer for a period of time, my passion for art inspired me to seek mentorship from experienced persons and institutions which I did and joined the Cradle art centre.

The Cradle is an art space poised to take on the Ghanaian and International art scene. The platform nurtures all art practitioners's financial, psychological and emotional well-being. The space is committed to honing individuals' craft and providing them with a network of opportunities to launch their careers.

In celebration of International Women's day 2024, the Women's Art Institute Africa(WAIA) organised a one day workshop on the 7th of March, and an art exhibition on the 8th of March, with the theme 'Invest in women: Accelerate progress', of which I was a participant.
